平成

HEISEI PERIOD

(1989 - PRESENT)

During the Heisei period (1989-present), Japan experienced the collapse of the bubble economy, followed by a prolonged period of economic stagnation known as the "lost decades." Political changes included the end of the cold war state, leading to the collapse of the longstanding Libperiodl Democratic Party dominance and the emergence of diverse coalition governments.

大正と昭和

TAISHO & SHOWA PERIODS

(1912 - 1989)

The Taisho period saw a shift towards democracy and economic challenges post-war, including the Great Kanto Earthquake. The Showa period witnessed significant turmoil, including economic instability, militarization, involvement in World War II, and subsequent occupation by GHQ. However, Japan underwent remarkable post-war recovery, transitioning into a major economic power characterized by rapid growth, political stability, and the emergence of the bubble economy in the 1980s.

江戸時代と明治時代

EDO & MEIJI PERIODS

(1603 - 1912)

The Edo period centralized power under the Tokugawa bakufu, fostering cultural growth despite social unrest. The Meiji period modernized Japan, establishing Western systems and expanding its global influence through victories and reforms.

戦国時代・安土桃山時代

PERIOD OF WARRING STATES
& AZUCHI-MOMOYAMA PERIOD

(1467 - 1603)

The Sengoku period featured powerful daimyo establishing regional control, transforming warfare with European trade and Christianity. Hideyoshi Toyotomi's reign in the Azuchi-Momoyama period unified Japan, fostering Momoyama culture.

南北朝・室町時代

NANBOKUCHO & MUROMACHI PERIODS

(1336 - 1590)

The Nanbokucho period witnessed Imperial Court division and cultural trends like extravagant behavior and poetry. The Muromachi period saw conflicts between the bakufu and Shugo daimyo, leading to the Sengoku period marked by social unrest and village community independence.

平安・鎌倉時代

HEIAN & KAMAKURA PERIODS

(794 - 1336)

Heian-kyo became the capital during the Heian period, fostering cultural blossoming and samurai influence. The Kamakura period saw the rise of the Kamakura bakufu, introducing military dominance and social changes like a monetary economy.

飛鳥・奈良時代

ASUKA & NARA PERIODS

(538 - 794)

In the Asuka period, Japan stabilized under Yamato sovereignty amid Imperial succession disputes, adopting Buddhism and implementing political reforms. The Nara period centralized power under Emperor Shomu, expanding the state system and patronizing cultural works while integrating neighboring regions like Silla and Emishi.

弥生・古墳時代

YAYOI & TUMULUS PERIODS

(300 BC - 538)

During the Yayoi period, Japan shifted to agriculture, spreading rice cultivation and building large settlements with hiperiodrchical structures. The Kofun period introduced keyhole-shaped tomb mounds under Yamato sovereignty, influenced by Korean and Chinese cultures.

旧石器時代・縄文時代

PALEOLITHIC & JOMON PERIODS

(Before 14,000 BC - 300 BC)

Between 30,000 to 100,000 years ago, early humans settled in Japan, initially adopting knife-type stone tools from Northern China. Microblades, introduced around 20,000 years ago from Siberia, gradually replaced knife-based tools, becoming widespread by 15,000 years ago. During the 12,000-year Jomon Period, Jomon-style earthenware emerged alongside fixed settlements like pit dwellings, and communities relied on hunting, fishing, and gathering. The end of the ice age around 12,000 years ago led to rising sea levels, isolating Japan and initiating the Jomon period, characterized by significant changes in lifestyle and culture. In smaller southeastern islands, life mirrored the Paleolithic period initially but evolved with the introduction of shell mound culture around 60,000 years ago, lasting until the end of the Heian period.
